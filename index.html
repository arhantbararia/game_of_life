The Rules of Life:

  1. Birth: A dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes a live cell.
  2. Survival: A live cell with two or three live neighbours survives to the next generation.
  3. Death: In all other cases, a cell dies or remains dead. Overpopulation (more than three neighbours) or loneliness (fewer than two) are fatal.

From these simple rules, astonishing structures can emerge some static, some oscillating, and some that travel across the grid like spaceships!
